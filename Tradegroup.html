Women Chamber of Commerce, Industry, Mines & Agriculture (WCCIMA)

Empowering women entrepreneurs and advancing gender-inclusive trade in Nigeria.

About WCCIMA

The Women Chamber of Commerce, Industry, Mines & Agriculture (WCCIMA) is a specialized arm of NACCIMA focused on empowering women in commerce, industry, mines, and agriculture. Established from the NACCIMA Business Women Group (NAWORG), it provides a strong platform for female entrepreneurs to thrive, advocate, and expand into global markets.

Core Objectives

Empowerment

Building capacity and confidence for women-owned businesses.

Advocacy

Driving policies that create equal opportunities for women entrepreneurs.

Trade Readiness

Preparing women for local and international trade opportunities, including AfCFTA.

Networking

Connecting women entrepreneurs with peers, mentors, and investors.

Impact Highlights

WCCIMA has made tangible contributions to advancing women entrepreneurs in Nigeria through capacity-building, advocacy, and strategic partnerships. Some of the key impacts include:

  • Trained over 100 women entrepreneurs for AfCFTA opportunities in partnership with UNDP.
  • Partnered with Premium Times to amplify women's voices in business.
  • Expanded a growing network of women professionals across Nigeria's commerce and industry sectors.

Partnerships & Collaborations

WCCIMA works with government agencies, development partners, and the private sector to expand opportunities for women. Key partners include:

  • UNDP – For trade readiness and entrepreneurship programs.
  • Media Partners – For visibility and awareness (e.g., Premium Times).

NACCIMA Youth Entrepreneurs (NYE)

Empowering Young Innovators & Business Leaders Across Nigeria

About NYE

The NACCIMA Youth Entrepreneurs (NYE) is the youth arm of NACCIMA dedicated to nurturing the next generation of business leaders in Nigeria. It provides a vibrant platform for young entrepreneurs to develop their skills, expand their networks, and contribute meaningfully to national economic growth.

Vision

To empower and motivate the youth to enable them participate effectively in social, political and economic development of the society

Mission

Create an enabling environment for youth empowerment and enhancement of employment opportunities and security.

Core Programs & Services

Trade Fairs & Exhibitions

Showcasing youth-led products and services to local and international markets.

Skill Acquisition & Capacity Building

Equipping young entrepreneurs with practical knowledge, tools, and mentorship to grow sustainable businesses.

Investment & Business Networking

Connecting youth with investors, partners, and business opportunities across Nigeria and beyond.

Innovation & Digital Programs

Supporting youth-driven innovation, technology adoption, and digital entrepreneurship.

How to Join

Joining NACCIMA means becoming part of a national network of business leaders, entrepreneurs, and innovators working together to shape Nigeria's economic future. Follow these simple steps to become a member today.

Step 1
Complete the Application Form

Fill out our membership form online or download a copy. This captures basic details about your business and ensures we place you in the right category.

Step 2
Submit Your Documents

Provide supporting documents such as CAC Registration and Tax ID. These help verify your business and confirm eligibility.

Step 3
Pay Membership Dues

Membership fees depend on your category. Once reviewed, you'll receive payment instructions with secure options for settlement.

Step 4
Confirmation & Welcome

After approval, you'll receive your Membership Certificate and login details for the Members' Portal. From here, you can access events, opportunities, and benefits.

Africa Continental Free Trade Agreement (AfCFTA) Trade Group

The AfCFTA Trade Group under NACCIMA drives Nigeria's participation in the African Continental Free Trade Agreement — fostering collaboration, advocacy, and innovation to strengthen regional trade and unlock growth across the continent.

About the AfCFTA Trade Group

The Africa Continental Free Trade Agreement (AfCFTA) Trade Group serves as NACCIMA's strategic platform for advancing Nigeria's position within Africa's largest single market initiative. It brings together private-sector leaders, policymakers, and trade professionals to ensure that Nigerian businesses benefit from the opportunities created by the removal of trade barriers across African borders.

The group focuses on promoting awareness of AfCFTA policies, supporting export readiness, and facilitating dialogue that connects local industries to continental trade frameworks. Through its activities, it contributes to a more competitive and integrated African economy led by Nigerian enterprise and innovation.

What We Do

Policy Advocacy

We engage government and regional bodies to shape trade policies that protect and promote the interests of Nigerian businesses under the AfCFTA framework.

Business Support

We provide guidance and resources to help companies expand into new African markets, ensuring compliance with continental trade requirements.

Capacity Development

We organize workshops, training sessions, and trade missions to equip entrepreneurs, MSMEs, and exporters with the knowledge to compete effectively across Africa.

Regional Collaboration

We build partnerships with African chambers, trade councils, and development organizations to drive collective growth and cooperation.
